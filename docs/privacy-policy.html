Privacy Policy

Milestones mobile application
Effective date: 26 June 2025
Last updated: 26 June 2025

This Privacy Policy describes how Milestones (“we”, “us”, or “our”) collects, uses, stores, shares, and protects information when you use the Milestones mobile application (“App”) and related services (collectively, the “Service”).

Milestones is a baby and child journey tracking application for parents and caregivers. The App is intended for use by adults. It is not directed at children to use on their own. Parents and caregivers enter information about their children.

By creating an account or using the Service, you agree to this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree, please do not use the Service.

1. Who operates Milestones

Milestones is operated by the developer of the Milestones application (the “Operator”). Android package name: com.milestones.app.

Privacy contact:

  • GitHub: github.com/tonkatrucka/milestones-app/issues (label your request “Privacy”)
  • Email: contact the Operator through the GitHub repository above to request a direct privacy email if needed.

2. Summary

  • We collect account credentials, child profile information you enter, care logs, photos, chat messages, and usage data needed to run the Service.
  • We use AI (Anthropic Claude) to power the conversational assistant and daily insights. Relevant text and summaries are sent to Anthropic for processing.
  • We host data on Supabase (database, authentication, file storage).
  • We do not sell your personal information.
  • We do not show ads in the App and do not use third-party advertising or analytics SDKs in the App.
  • Family sharing: owners may invite other adults as caregivers or viewers for a child profile.
  • Photos you upload are stored in cloud storage buckets configured for public read access via URL. Treat shared links accordingly.

3.3 Care and activity logs

You or invited caregivers may log daily care events, including:

  • Nappy / diaper changes — type (wet, dirty, both, dry), time, optional notes
  • Meals and feeds — type (breast, bottle, solid, snack), optional amount (ml), food description, time, notes
  • Sleep — sleep start and end times, duration, notes
  • Metadata — structured fields stored as JSON where applicable (e.g. meal amounts)

Each log is associated with a child profile and the user who created it where applicable.

3.5 Photos and media

You may upload photos from your device camera or photo library for:

  • Child avatars
  • Milestone and memory records
  • Chat messages to the assistant

Supported formats include JPEG, PNG, WebP, and HEIC, with a per-file size limit of 10 MB on our storage backend.

Important — public media URLs: Photos uploaded to our cloud storage are stored in buckets configured for public read access. Anyone with the direct URL may be able to view the image. URLs are not broadly published by us, but they are not secret links. Do not upload images you would not want someone with the link to see.

The assistant may create or update records in your account (e.g. log a nappy change) based on your instructions. Only the most recent portion of conversation context is sent to the AI provider per request (limited message window).

3.7 Insights and research content

  • Daily insights — We generate short and long observations about patterns in your child’s logged activity (e.g. sleep, feeding). Generation uses aggregated statistics derived from your logs, your child’s name and age, and Anthropic’s language models. Results are cached per child per calendar day.
  • Research bullets — The App may show educational bullet points sourced from a pre-generated research bank (government and health-authority publishers). Selection uses your child’s age bracket, activity categories, device region (see below), and novelty tracking (which bullets were previously shown). Research bullets do not contain your personal data; we store which bullet IDs were shown to each child profile.
  • Region preference — We read your device’s region/locale code (via expo-localization) to prefer region-relevant research sources where available.

3.8 Family sharing and invitations

If you are a child profile owner, you may:

  • Invite other users by email address with a role of caregiver or viewer
  • Generate invite links containing a unique token
  • Manage team membership and roles

Invited users who accept gain access to the child’s shared data according to their role. Caregivers can add and edit most content; viewers have read-only access. Invite records include email, role, token, expiry (7 days by default), and acceptance timestamp.

5. AI processing and automated features

Milestones uses Anthropic Claude language models for the conversational assistant and for generating narrative insights. When you use these features, we send relevant content to Anthropic’s API, which may include:

  • Your chat messages and recent conversation context
  • Child first name, date of birth / derived age, and current date
  • Statistical summaries of logged events (counts, durations, week-over-week comparisons)
  • Image URLs or image content for photos you attach in chat

AI outputs are generated automatically. They are intended for general informational and organizational support only. They are not medical advice. Always consult a qualified health professional for medical concerns.

Anthropic processes data according to its own terms and privacy policy. We configure API access server-side; your Anthropic API credentials are not embedded in the App distributed to users.

8. Data retention

  • Account data — retained while your account is active
  • Child profiles, logs, milestones, memories, chat — retained until you or an authorized user deletes them or deletes the child profile, or until account deletion
  • Insights cache — stored per child per day; updated when regenerated

  • Invites — retained until accepted, revoked, expired, or deleted
  • Backups — residual copies may persist for a limited period in backup systems

When records are deleted from the App, associated database rows are removed subject to our backend configuration. Deleting media from records may not immediately purge all CDN or backup copies everywhere in the infrastructure.

10. Security

We implement technical and organizational measures appropriate to the nature of the data, including:

No method of transmission or storage is 100% secure. You are responsible for keeping your password confidential and for the security of your device.
